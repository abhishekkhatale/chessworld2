1. Basic Rules of Chess

Objective:

  • Win by checkmating the opponent's king: The king is in check, and there is no legal move to escape the check.

2. Board Setup

  • 8x8 Grid (64 squares):
    • White pieces start on ranks 1 and 2.
    • Black pieces start on ranks 7 and 8.
  • Pieces:
    • 1 King
    • 1 Queen
    • 2 Rooks
    • 2 Bishops
    • 2 Knights
    • 8 Pawns

Initial Placement:

  • Rooks: Corners (a1, h1 for White; a8, h8 for Black).
  • Knights: Next to rooks.
  • Bishops: Next to knights.
  • Queen: On her color (White queen on a white square, Black queen on black square).
  • King: Beside the queen.
  • Pawns: Frontline (rank 2 for White, rank 7 for Black).

3. How Pieces Move

King:

  • Moves one square in any direction.
  • Cannot move into check.
  • Castling: Special move involving king and rook (details below).

Queen:

  • Moves any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

Rook:

  • Moves any number of squares horizontally or vertically.

Bishop:

  • Moves any number of squares diagonally.

Knight:

  • Moves in an L-shape: Two squares in one direction and one square perpendicular.
  • Can jump over other pieces.

Pawn:

  • Moves one square forward.
  • Can move two squares forward on its first move.
  • Capturing: Diagonally forward (not straight).
  • Promotion: When a pawn reaches the last rank (rank 8 for White, rank 1 for Black), it promotes to a queen, rook, bishop, or knight.
  • En Passant: Special capture if an opponent's pawn moves two squares forward.

4. Special Moves

Castling:

  • A move that involves the king and a rook:
    • King moves two squares toward the rook.
    • Rook moves next to the king on the other side.
  • Conditions:
    1. No pieces between the king and rook.
    2. King and rook must not have moved earlier.
    3. The king cannot be in check, move through check, or end in check.

En Passant:

  • When an opponent's pawn moves two squares forward (from its starting position), it can be captured as if it had moved only one square.
  • This move must happen immediately after the pawn moves.

Pawn Promotion:

  • A pawn reaching the opposite end of the board must promote to:
    • Queen (most common), Rook, Bishop, or Knight.

5. Check, Checkmate, and Stalemate

Check:

  • The king is under threat of capture.
  • The player must respond to check:
    1. Move the king.
    2. Block the check.
    3. Capture the attacking piece.

Checkmate:

  • The king is in check, and there is no legal move to escape.

Stalemate:

  • The player has no legal moves, but the king is not in check.
  • Result: Draw.

6. Draw Conditions

  1. Stalemate: No legal moves, but the king is safe.
  2. Insufficient Material: Not enough pieces to checkmate (e.g., king vs. king).
  3. Threefold Repetition: The same position occurs 3 times.
  4. 50-Move Rule: No pawn moves or captures in 50 consecutive moves.
  5. Mutual Agreement: Both players agree to a draw.

7. Single Player (vs Bot)

  • You play against an AI or bot.
  • Difficulty levels vary (easy, medium, hard, etc.).
  • The bot uses algorithms to calculate moves.

8. Double Player (vs Human)

  • Players alternate moves.
  • Follow the same rules.
  • Decide:
    • Who plays White (White always moves first).
    • Use a timer for timed games (optional).

9. Winning the Game

  • Checkmate the opponent's king.
  • Opponent resigns (gives up).
  • Opponent runs out of time (if using a timer).

10. Optional Game Modes

  • Blitz Chess: Very short time limits (e.g., 3 minutes each).
  • Rapid Chess: Moderate time limits (10-30 minutes).
  • Classical Chess: Longer games (over 30 minutes).
  • Bullet Chess: Ultra-fast (1 minute or less).
